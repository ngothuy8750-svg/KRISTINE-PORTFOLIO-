Hello, I'm Kristine

I’m a Toronto-based graphic designer currently studying at George Brown College’s School of Design. I’m passionate about creating branding, motion, and digital designs that feel clear, meaningful, and approachable. I enjoy translating ideas into visual experiences that communicate purpose and personality. My work spans identity design, posters, editorial layouts, and digital campaigns, with a strong emphasis on concept-driven outcomes. I approach each project with careful attention to typography, intentional colour choices, and clean, structured compositions. Through my coursework, I’ve gained hands-on experience in branding systems, packaging, web design, and print production, allowing me to work confidently across both digital and physical formats. I value consistency, clarity, and storytelling in every design decision.

About

Branding

Branding is the process of shaping how a business is perceived by its audience. It involves creating a consistent visual identity, including logos, colours, typography, and imagery, as well as defining the brand’s voice, values, and message

Logo

A logo designer is responsible for creating a visual mark that represents a brand’s identity. Their work involves researching the brand, understanding its values and target audience, developing concepts, and designing a logo that is simple, memorable

Digital Designs

Digital design involves creating visual content for digital platforms such as websites, social media, apps, and online advertisements. It focuses on combining layout, typography, colour, and imagery to communicate messages clearly and engage users through screens.
